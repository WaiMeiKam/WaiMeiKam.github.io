<Problem>

What was broken, slow, or missing. The user or business pain. Context on how the problem accumulated — who owned what, what had been tried before.

Keep this grounded in specifics. No vague "the product needed improvement" — say what was actually broken and what it cost.

</Problem>

<Process>

### Research phase

What you did to understand the problem before designing. Methods, participants, key findings. What surprised you.

### Design approach

The core structural decisions. Not every iteration — the logic of the approach.

### Prototyping and testing

How you validated. What variants you tested and why. What the data said.

</Process>

<KeyDecision title="The decision title">

The hardest call. The tradeoff that wasn't obvious. The stakeholder conversation that could have gone badly.

Why you made the call you did. What you would have needed to make a different one.

</KeyDecision>

<Outcome>

What changed. Metrics where you have them. Qualitative shifts where you don't. What the team learned.

Be specific. "Improved conversion" is not an outcome. "23% → 68% sign-up completion" is.

</Outcome>

<Reflection>

If you did it again: what you'd do earlier, harder, differently. What you'd double down on.

One honest thing you'd change. One thing that worked better than expected.

</Reflection>
